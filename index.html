Source-Filtler HiFiGAN: Fast and Pitch Controllable High-Fidelity Neural Vocoder

Reo Yoneyama1, Yi-Chiao Wu2, Tomoki Toda1

1Nagoya University, Japan, 2Meta Reality Labs Research, USA

Submitted to ICASSP 2023

Abstract

A Neural vocoder is crucial for the performance of voice generation applications. In addition to voice quality, voice controllability and synthesis speed are also essential features of a neural vocoder. In particular, controlling fundamental frequency (F0) is especially important for accurately reproducing intonation and musical notation. Although our previous work, unified source-filter GAN vocoder, simultaneously achieves high voice quality and F0 controllability, the very high-temporal resolution inputs result in high computation cost. The consequent slow synthesis issue makes it challenging for real-time applications. On the other hand, although the up-sampling-based vocoders such as HiFiGAN achieve fast high-fidelity voice generation, their pitch-agnostic architectures usually lack F0 controllability. This paper proposes a fast and F0 controllable high-fidelity neural vocoder by introducing the source-filter theory into HiFiGAN. According to the experimental results, the proposed method outperforms WORLD and HiFiGAN on Japanese singing voice analysis-synthesis while achieving better speech quality and faster synthesis speed than HiFiGAN on a single CPU.

Demo

Only Namine Ritsu [1] database is used for the training. All vocoders are conditioned with WORLD [2] features extracted with same hyperparameters. All provided samples are unseen songs in the training.
Baseline models

  • WORLD : A conventional source-filter vocoder [2].
  • hn-uSFGAN : Harmonic-plus-noise unified source-filter GAN [3].
  • HiFiGAN w/ Sine : HiFiGAN [4] w/ sine with sine embedding [5, 6].
Ablation models
  • SiFiGAN w/o reg : SiFiGAN trained without the regularization loss on output source excitation signals [3].
  • SiFiGAN w/ F0 : SiFiGAN input F0 directry to the filter-branch.
  • SiFiGAN const : SiFiGAN trained with a constant dense factor [7] for all PDCNNs.
  • SiFiGAN single : SiFiGAN without separation of the source and filter branches.
